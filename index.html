Design and Layout of a Temperature-Dependent Voltage Reference Using P-Channel JFETs and Diodes

  • Designed an integrated circuit device to generate a temperature-proportional voltage (VPTAT) using pn-junction diode behavior.
  • Implemented diodes (D3, D4) using base and emitter regions of an IC NPN process to achieve temperature-dependent forward voltage characteristics.
  • Utilized p-channel JFETs (J1, J2, J4, J5) to maintain consistent bias current ratios across the diodes for output voltage accuracy.
  • Calculated voltage difference (ΔVD3,D4) using Shockley's diode equation, showing linear dependence on absolute temperature.
  • Computed built-in potential (Vbi) and reverse saturation current (Is) for the diode layout using process-specific doping levels and carrier lifetimes.
  • Designed the layout and calculated pinch-off voltage (Vp) and zero-bias conductivity for the p-channel JFETs using semiconductor device physics.
  • Designed bias resistors (R2 and R3) using base diffusion, selecting geometry and material resistivity to bias J1 gate at 5V.
  • Implemented a feedback loop using an opamp to control JFET gates, minimizing bias ratio errors and improving VPTAT stability.
  • Explored area scaling by using larger layouts for D2, D6, D7, and J2/J4/J5 to reduce parasitic effects and improve thermal response.
  • Analyzed advantages of area scaling (e.g., thermal performance, layout efficiency) and considered trade-offs such as increased parasitic capacitance.

Hybrid Power Generation System

  • Uses a boost converter, battery, inverter, and control electronics to generate and distribute electricity
  • Provides a solution for pollution reduction, energy needs in remote areas, and reduces reliance on non-renewable sources
  • Surplus power can be supplied to local households or the electric grid
  • The roof ventilator generates mechanical energy through airflow, which is converted into electricity using a dynamo and gears
  • A rectifier converts the AC output from the dynamo to DC
  • Solar panels are mounted on a dual-axis tracker to maximize sunlight exposure and efficiency
  • The boost converter raises voltage to a sufficient level to charge the battery through a battery charger
  • An inverter with overload protection powers the AC loads
  • A relay circuit manages the power flow between the boost converter, battery, and inverter
  • The one of the relay (say 1) disconnects the charger once the battery is fully charged to prevent overcharging
  • Relay (say 2) Operation Modes: CASE 1: (Boost Converter → Charger & Battery → Relay → Inverter → Load) When power from solar and ventilator is available, the boost converter charges the battery and supplies the inverter CASE 2: (Battery → Relay → Inverter → Load) When there is low or no generation, the battery alone supplies the inverter to power the load
  • Supports sustainable energy usage for future generations.
